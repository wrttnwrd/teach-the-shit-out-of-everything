# Brief: The First Hour Of A Wizard's Crisis

**Answers questions from questions.md:** Q13 (first-hour response, currently course-only), Q29 (designated spokesperson), Q30 (holding statements), Q31 (internal-first comms).

**Size / type:** Teach-small · Upgrade (the course owns this content; no public standalone piece does).

## Reader

A practicing wizard who has read `post-cow.md`, sees themselves in Archibald, has no designated spokesperson, has never drafted a holding statement, and has only a rough idea who in the household is supposed to do what when the green starts filling up.

## Concrete problem manifestations

- Your apprentice runs in: "There's a crowd on the green."
- A bailiff's messenger arrives and you have no prepared reply.
- Six hours from now this could be real and you don't know who's in charge of what.

## Brand-agnostic teaching (biggest part)

- What "Golden Hour" means operationally — the first frame hardens into fact, and you win this window in advance or not at all.
- Pre-crisis work you can do this afternoon:
    - Pick a spokesperson who isn't you.
    - Draft three short holding statements for your three likeliest crises.
    - Write a one-sentence household brief you'd give before briefing anyone outside.
- In-crisis sequence: ensure safety; stand up a command center (physical or sending-stone hub); don't answer specific allegations without facts; brief internal before external.
- Archibald's three bad decisions as worked examples:
    - Silence confirms absurd accusations.
    - Explanation extends a panic.
    - Casting a spell in front of scared villagers looks like an attack.
