# Brief: Sending-Stone Hygiene

**Answers questions from questions.md:** Q18 (sending-stone communications protection), and in part Q4 (how the spoofer got Halric's cousin's private joke).

**Size / type:** Teach-small · New stuff (the infosec page names "warded communications" as a service without ever teaching the basics).

## Reader

Any working practitioner with ongoing family, apprentice-away, or cross-guild correspondence. Specifically: anyone whose authentication is a private joke that's been in use unchanged for years.

## Concrete problem manifestations

- A sending reads exactly like your cousin — same cadence, same joke — but something's off.
- Your apprentice is at the academy and you need to confirm a message supposedly from them.
- You realize your authentication signal has been the same since you set it.

## Brand-agnostic teaching (biggest part)

- **What authentication actually is:** a signal only two people know, on a channel other than the one it's authenticating. Jokes said at weddings don't qualify.
- **Rotation:** set signals in person, on a single trip, nowhere near a sending stone. Change periodically. Assume anything older than a year is leaked.
- **Layering:** one signal per sensitive correspondent, not one for all. A leak on your cousin's side shouldn't compromise your apprentice.
- **Behavior under suspicion:** if the signal is right but the message feels wrong, don't respond. Verify on a different channel. Five-minute delays cost nothing; wrong replies can cost everything.
- **Detecting leaks:** pre-seed a decoy signal only a leaker would try. If it's ever used, you've confirmed the leak.

## What to avoid

- Crypto jargon.
- Pretending this is solved by technology — it isn't, and the honesty is load-bearing.
- Framing rotation as paranoia. It's hygiene, like washing a scrying bowl between uses.
