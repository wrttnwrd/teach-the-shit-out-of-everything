# Call Transcript — 001
**Date:** Frostmoon 16
**Type:** Existing customer · Quarterly check-in
**Caller:** Archibald (redacted surname), Transmuter · PR retainer client since Frostmoon 02

**MIRELLA:** Archibald. How's the tower?

**ARCHIBALD:** Standing. That still feels worth saying.

**MIRELLA:** It will for a while. What can I do for you?

**ARCHIBALD:** The song.

**MIRELLA:** The good one or the bad one?

**ARCHIBALD:** The good one. Your bard. The sick-goat song. It's — people are still singing it. At the tavern. It's been nine weeks.

**MIRELLA:** That's the idea, yes.

**ARCHIBALD:** No, I know. I understand that's the idea. The thing is — (pause) — I'm now being stopped on the road by people who want to tell me about their goats. Their actual goats. I have been invited to inspect three goats this week. One of them wasn't even sick.

**MIRELLA:** (laughs) That is a new one.

**ARCHIBALD:** Is it a problem?

**MIRELLA:** Not a bad one. It's a sign the frame stuck. But I hear you — you didn't sign up to be the regional goat specialist.

**ARCHIBALD:** I did not.

**MIRELLA:** Two options. One, we extend the song's range. Get it sung in the next three villages over. Dilutes the concentration in your immediate area. Two, we start seeding a second story — something that's equally positive but isn't about goats. Healing. Harvest. A different small good deed. Over about six weeks, the goat thing fades.

**ARCHIBALD:** Which do you recommend?

**MIRELLA:** The second. The first is cheaper but it just moves the goats around. The second actually solves the problem.

**ARCHIBALD:** Fine. Do it.

**ARCHIBALD:** One other thing. _[⚑ Gap question]_ My sister is — she's also a practitioner, she's in the Free Cities. She had an incident. Not a cow one. Something different. She wanted to know if you work outside the Sword Coast. Can I send her your way?

**MIRELLA:** (pause) Honestly, that's — we don't have a formal answer for that. We've done work as far as Neverwinter and as far south as Baldur's Gate. The Free Cities is further than any active engagement we have. The honest answer is it depends on what her incident is and how urgent. If it's urgent, no — we can't be there in the window that matters. If it's longer-cycle reputation work, we could probably make it function, but the travel costs are going to change the economics.

**ARCHIBALD:** So maybe.

**MIRELLA:** Maybe. Have her send a note to intake describing the situation and I'll give her an honest read on whether we're the right firm. If we're not, I know two people in the Free Cities who are, and I'll refer her.

**ARCHIBALD:** You'd refer out?

**MIRELLA:** If we can't serve her well, yes. Half-serving her is worse than not serving her.

**ARCHIBALD:** Alright. I'll tell her.

**MIRELLA:** Anything else?

**ARCHIBALD:** No. Well — thank you. For the nine weeks, and the goats, and — everything.

**MIRELLA:** Keep your tower standing, Archibald.

**ARCHIBALD:** I'm trying.
