# Call Transcript — 002
**Date:** Frostmoon 11
**Type:** Existing customer · Incident follow-up
**Caller:** Delara Venn, Abjurer · Physical Security retainer since Rainmoon 03
**GSE rep:** Corrin Vale, Physical Security Lead
**Duration:** 31 minutes
**Recorded with consent:** Yes

**CORRIN:** Delara. I got your sending. What happened?

**DELARA:** Someone was on the east wall last night. Around third watch. The ward triggered, the detail responded, whoever it was got down the wall and into the treeline before we could get eyes on them.

**CORRIN:** No contact?

**DELARA:** No contact. Ward worked. Detail worked. They were just — faster than we were.

**CORRIN:** That's good news, mostly. Ward and response did their job. But I hear the question underneath.

**DELARA:** Yes. If they'd been a hair faster or a hair more committed, they'd have been inside. What do we do different?

**CORRIN:** A few things. First, I want our team back out tomorrow to re-walk the east approach. If someone got to the wall unobserved, there's a gap in our outer perimeter I didn't see the first time. We'll find it.

**DELARA:** Good.

**CORRIN:** Second, we talked about a second ring of lower-threshold wards about thirty paces out from the wall. You declined. I'd like to revisit that.

**DELARA:** Fine, revisit it. Send me numbers.

**CORRIN:** I will. Third — and this is the harder one — our response time last night was four and a half minutes. That's within spec but on the slow end. I want to talk about whether we need a two-person standing watch instead of one-plus-on-call.

**DELARA:** That's a lot more expensive.

**CORRIN:** It is. Roughly seventy percent more, on the guard line.

**DELARA:** Let me think about it.

**CORRIN:** Of course.

**DELARA:** _[⚑ Gap question]_ Corrin. Something else. If one of your guards is injured on my site — seriously injured, or killed — what happens? Who's liable, what happens to their family, how does that work?

**CORRIN:** (pause) That's a fair question and I want to be honest with you: I don't know the full answer. Here's what I know. All our guards sign a hazard acknowledgment at hire — they understand the work. We carry an indemnity fund for serious injury and a death benefit for their families, which we pay regardless of how the incident happened. The amount is — I'd have to check. It's a fixed sum, not tied to the client contract.

**DELARA:** And my exposure?

**CORRIN:** In general, if the guard was doing their job, none. If the incident was because of something you did or failed to disclose — like, for instance, a ward you didn't tell us about that killed them — then the contract has a clause about that and yes, there's exposure. But the default answer is you're not liable for the risks of the work itself. That's on us.

**DELARA:** The indemnity fund — have you ever paid out?

**CORRIN:** Twice in our history.

**DELARA:** And the families were taken care of?

**CORRIN:** By our standards, yes. Whether they'd say so — I hope so. I know we're still in touch with both of them.

**DELARA:** Can you send me the actual numbers? The death benefit, the indemnity terms?

**CORRIN:** I'll have our operations lead put together a one-page document. Should be with you within three days.

**DELARA:** Thank you.

**CORRIN:** Anything else on last night?

**DELARA:** No. Go find the gap.

**CORRIN:** Going now.
