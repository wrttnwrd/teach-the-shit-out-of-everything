# Mirella Thane

Head of Public Relations. Thirty years in reputation management. Writer of the cow-incident playbook we wish we didn't need.

> **TL;DR** Mirella runs our PR practice. Before Green Slaad, she was a court bard, a royal herald, and the person three noble houses quietly called when a scandal needed to disappear. She's run point on every major cow incident we've handled and written most of what we teach.

If you've hired our PR service in the last fifteen years, Mirella either ran your engagement or trained the person who did. She is the reason our cow-incident response time is measured in hours and our recovery rate is what it is.[^1]

## Current role

Mirella has led the Green Slaad Public Relations practice since its founding. She's responsible for:

- **Crisis response.** She or one of her seconds is in the saddle within an hour of any active incident.
- **Reputation audits.** She designed our methodology and still personally runs audits for new clients above a certain threshold of risk.
- **Bard retainer program.** She recruits, trains, and tunes the network of bards who handle ongoing narrative work in our client regions.
- **The training curriculum.** Everything we teach apprentices and staff about "what to say at the tavern" started as her lecture notes.

## Before Green Slaad

### Court bard to House Vareth (8 years)

Mirella started as a working musician. She took the court-bard post at sixteen, which is unusually young, and held it through three successions and two wars of contested inheritance. Her job title said "bard." Her actual role was what we would now call chief communications officer — she drafted the official announcements, briefed the heralds, and managed what the countryside was told and in what order.

She left House Vareth after declining to lie about the circumstances of a duke's death. The house survived the truth. She considers this the most important lesson of her early career.[^2]

### Royal herald, Kingdom of Aldoran (6 years)

As royal herald, Mirella was responsible for official proclamations across seventeen provinces. She built the messenger network that carried them, negotiated the protocols for cross-border announcements with four neighboring kingdoms, and personally delivered the coronation proclamation of Queen Aethelind — which, because of a disputed succession, had to be announced in three cities simultaneously by three different heralds reading from identical scripts. Her scripts.

It was during this period that she developed the approach to _narrative timing_ that we still use: the observation that what people hear _first_ becomes the frame, and everything they hear afterward gets fitted to that frame. You cannot unmake a first impression. You can only race to establish it yourself.

### Independent consultant (11 years)

After leaving royal service, Mirella spent eleven years as a private consultant to noble houses and, increasingly, to individual practitioners of the arcane arts. Her client list from this period is confidential — this is a professional requirement, not a marketing tactic — but the pattern of her work is well-known in the industry:

- Three separate noble heirs accused of poisoning rivals. All three cleared in public opinion within a season. None required a trial.
- A prominent abjurer accused of summoning a fiend in the marketplace. (He hadn't — the fiend was an illusion cast _at_ him by a rival.) Mirella produced the rival within two weeks.
- The Lady Castellan of a free city, accused of using arcane means to fix a grain-price dispute. The accusation was, embarrassingly, correct. Mirella did not deny it. She reframed it as price stabilization during a famine. The Lady Castellan kept her position.[^3]

She came to Green Slaad fifteen years ago because, in her words, "the wizards needed it more, and the noble houses had each other to lean on."

## Writing and teaching

Mirella has written most of our internal doctrine and a fair amount of what we publish. Notable pieces:

- _The Cow-Incident Playbook._ Seventeen pages. Internal document. Updated annually whether we need to or not.
- _The Three Frames._ Her core teaching on how accusations get shaped and reshaped in public memory. Required reading for all PR staff.
- _On Not Lying._ A short monograph arguing that spin based on falsehood always collapses, usually at the worst possible moment. Distributed free to clients.
- _Tavern Listening._ The curriculum we teach bards joining our retainer program. She wrote it. She still teaches the opening session personally.

She also lectures twice a year at the College of Arms and once a year at the Guild of Heralds, though she insists these are favors to old colleagues and not part of her job.

## How she works

A few things clients tend to notice, in case you're deciding whether she's a fit:

- **She will not lie for you.** She will find the true thing worth telling, and she will make sure it's told well and first. If the true thing is genuinely bad, she will tell you that, and she will tell you what to do about it.
- **She runs crises calmly.** Several clients have remarked on this. The mob is at the gate and she is asking about the miller's gout. This is not detachment. It is a correctly calibrated sense of which details matter.
- **She is direct.** If your first three instincts in a crisis are wrong, she will say so. Clients who cannot take this find her difficult. Clients who can find her indispensable.[^4]
- **She reads everything.** Before she meets you, she has read your published work, the regional gossip, the last three years of local proclamations, and probably a ballad or two. She will know things about your reputation you did not know yourself.
