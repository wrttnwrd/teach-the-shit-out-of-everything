# Physical Security

Bodyguards, wards, and people who arrive before the torches do.

> **TL;DR** We stop the parts of the world that want to hit you with a thing from hitting you with a thing. Armed humans, enchanted perimeters, and an incident response team on retainer.

You can un-make a goblin at forty paces, sure. Can you do it at forty paces when you're asleep, and the goblin brought seventeen friends, and one of them is a silenced rogue with a crossbow already aimed at your reading nook?[^1]

Right. That's what this service is for.

## What's included

| Service | What it actually does |
|---|---|
| Personal bodyguard detail | One to four guards, cycled on twelve-hour shifts. All of them were adventurers. All of them survived their own adventures, which is the resume criterion that matters. |
| Tower warding | Layered _alarm_, _glyph of warding_, and custom trigger-chains. We calibrate to **your** daily routine so you don't trip your own wards at breakfast. |
| Sanctum hardening | Physical reinforcement of doors, windows, and the one secret passage you forgot about. We always find a secret passage. There is always a secret passage. |
| Travel escort | For when you _must_ leave the tower. Mounts, routes, decoy caravans. We'll teleport you if we have to, but we'd rather not — teleport sickness is real and unflattering. |
| Rapid response | Sending stone linked to our on-call team. Average response time: eleven minutes. Faster than most fire brigades. Considerably. |

## How we work

We don't sell packages. Every wizard's threat surface is different, and I won't charge you for a bodyguard detail if your actual problem is that your familiar keeps opening the window.

Our standard engagement:

1. **Threat assessment.** Two days on-site. We walk the tower, meet the staff, read the omens, talk to the villagers.[^2]
2. **Recommendation brief.** Plain-language, no jargon, no upsells. You'll see exactly what we think and exactly what it costs.
3. **Deployment.** Guards on rotation, wards laid, response line open.
4. **Quarterly review.** Threats change. So do we.

## What we won't do

I want to be direct about this, because the industry has a reputation problem:

- **We don't use expendables.** If someone swears to take an arrow for you, we'd prefer they also swear to dodge it.
- **We don't hire necromancers.** Undead guards are cheap and unreliable, and they're a PR disaster. See our [PR service](public-relations.md), which we'd rather not have to run on ourselves.
- **We don't guarantee against dragons.** No one can. Anyone who says otherwise is lying or about to die.
