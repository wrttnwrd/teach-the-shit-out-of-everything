# The Cow Incident

**Summary**: GSE's defining case study. A wizard ([[archibald]]) was falsely accused of turning a village child into a cow. His three instinctive responses all escalated the crisis. GSE's intervention — led by [[mirella-thane]] — saved the tower in hours.

**Sources**: gse-site/post-cow.md, gse-site/public-relations.md, gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

## Timeline

### Hour 0 — The accusation

A farmer's eleven-year-old daughter went missing for an afternoon. She turned up at supper, fine. Separately, an unrecognized cow was seen near Archibald's tower that morning. By dusk, the miller's wife connected the two events. By full dark, it was the only story in the tavern. By morning, it was treated as fact. (source: gse-site/post-cow.md)

The actual situation: Archibald was trying to turn *himself* into a cow, for research. Entirely different. GSE did not share this with the villagers. (source: gse-site/public-relations.md)

### Bad decision 1: Refuse to engage (Hour 0)

When a concerned neighbor came to the door, Archibald refused to open it. His logic: the accusation was absurd, so it didn't merit a response.

**The lesson**: The more absurd the accusation, the more important it is to respond. Reasonable accusations burn out. Ridiculous ones get set to music. By the third night, there was a song. (source: gse-site/post-cow.md)

### Bad decision 2: Explain transmutation theory (Hour 18)

Archibald came to the village green and explained the schools of magic, material component costs, the difference between transformation and illusion, and offered to produce his component logbook.

**What the villagers heard**: "I have a book. In the book, I record every time I do cow magic." (source: gse-site/post-cow.md)

### Bad decision 3: Cast a light spell (Hour 26)

At peak crisis — mob formed, torches produced, blacksmith's hammer involved — Archibald cast dancing lights to illuminate the courtyard as a gesture of openness.

**What the villagers saw**: A wizard casting a spell at them. The situation worsened immediately. (source: gse-site/post-cow.md)

### Good decision: Call GSE (Hour 24, arrival Hour 27)

Archibald's apprentice sent for GSE at hour 24 (more sense than her master). Response team arrived at hour 27 — would have been faster but Archibald's wards would have killed a teleportation arrival. GSE has since updated intake to collect ward-calibration data. (source: gse-site/post-cow.md)

## The GSE response

[[mirella-thane]] arrived and gave Archibald three instructions: "Stop talking. Go inside. Do not come out until I tell you to." He complied. This is described as the only reason this is a case study and not an obituary.

The response, in order:

1. **Produce the child.** Confirmed en route that the girl was home and safe. Asked the family to bring her to the courtyard. She was visibly not a cow.
2. **Acknowledge the fear.** Mirella said publicly: "We understand how this looked. We'd have been worried too." This cost nothing and removed ~90% of the crowd's heat. Key principle: acknowledge the fear, not the accusation.
3. **Pay reparations.** Paid the miller for his troubles. Paid the blacksmith for a hammer (he hadn't lost one). The gesture is symbolic — a ritual of reparation, not an admission of fault.
4. **Change the song.** A bard was in the tavern by the next night with a new song about Archibald healing a sick goat the previous year (verified from his logs). The new narrative was in the air before the old one finished traveling.

(source: gse-site/post-cow.md)

## Cost

- Total spend: ~6 silver in reparations, standard crisis retainer, four nights of bard time.
- Total cost of not calling: one tower, one library, one wizard.

"We have this conversation a lot. The math is always this stupid." (source: gse-site/post-cow.md)

## Legacy

The cow incident generated:

- **The Cow-Incident Playbook** — 17 pages, internal document, updated annually. Written by Mirella the night of the incident. (source: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md)
- **The published case study** — With Archibald's permission, anonymized. Used as the anchor for GSE's [[public-relations]] service page. (source: gse-site/post-cow.md)
- **The goat song** — The narrative displacement song became so successful it created its own problem. Nine weeks later, villagers were still singing it and inviting Archibald to inspect their goats. See [[archibald]] for the follow-up. (source: call-01-customer-pr-archibald.md)
- **Lessons 02, 03, and 04 of the [[crisis-pr-course]]** — Draw directly from this incident. (source: gse-site/course-crisis-pr.md)

GSE has handled twenty-three cow incidents total. (source: gse-site/public-relations.md)

## Principles demonstrated

- **Respond to absurd accusations immediately** — silence confirms them.
- **Stop explaining; produce evidence** — the child, not the component logbook.
- **Acknowledge the fear, not the accusation** — two different things.
- **[[narrative-timing]]** — what people hear first becomes the frame.
- **Reparation as ritual** — paying for the hammer that wasn't broken.
- **The [[golden-hour]] is won in advance** — Archibald had no spokesperson, no holding statement, no internal crisis protocol. The apprentice calling GSE was the only pre-staged action that worked, and it was informal.
