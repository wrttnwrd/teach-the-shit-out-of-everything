# Operational Gaps

**Summary**: Five structural and procedural gaps identified across GSE's call transcripts. Three are structural (policy, playbook, product) and two are procedural (reference docs, consistent messaging). The structural ones are more urgent because each successive call keeps hitting them.

**Sources**: gse-calls/README.md, call-01-customer-pr-archibald.md, call-02-customer-physical-delara.md, call-03-customer-infosec-halric.md, call-04-prospect-physical-iolanthe.md, call-05-prospect-pr-northwatch.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

## Gap 1: Geographic coverage policy (structural)

**Surface**: Call 001 — [[archibald]] asked if GSE works outside the Sword Coast for his sister in the Free Cities.

**Problem**: No written policy on out-of-region engagements. No tiered travel pricing. No vetted referral list. [[mirella-thane]] cited two referral names from personal knowledge — nothing documented.

**Current range**: Engagements documented as far as Neverwinter (north) and Baldur's Gate (south). The Free Cities would be the furthest engagement attempted.

**Recommendation from rep notes**: Standing internal doc on geographic coverage + referral partners.

(source: call-01-customer-pr-archibald.md)

## Gap 2: Guard indemnity and death-benefit reference (procedural)

**Surface**: Call 002 — [[delara-venn]] asked about liability and benefits if a guard is injured or killed on site.

**Problem**: [[corrin-vale]] could not quote specific death-benefit or indemnity fund figures. Had to fall back on "I'll check." Every physical-security lead should be able to cite these from memory.

**What is known**: Hazard acknowledgment at hire. Indemnity fund exists. Death benefit exists (paid regardless of cause). Two payouts in GSE history. Client is not liable if guard was doing their job — exposure only if client failed to disclose hazards.

**Recommendation from rep notes**: Operations to produce a one-page reference card with current figures, next-of-kin protocol, and standard contract clause language. Needed permanently.

(source: call-02-customer-physical-delara.md)

## Gap 3: Insider-threat playbook (structural)

**Surface**: Call 003 — [[halric-bren]]'s apprentice appears to be reading his preparation notes.

**Problem**: GSE has handled approximately six confirmed insider-leak cases with mixed outcomes and no written retrospective. Every engagement is first-principles work. The diagnostic side (decoys, monitoring) works. The resolution side (what to do once confirmed) has no documented methodology.

**Cross-service gap**: Resolution requires both [[information-security]] (detection) and [[public-relations]] (relationship management, confrontation strategy, separation terms). Currently these are not integrated for this use case.

**Recommendation from rep notes**: Internal project to write up the six prior cases, surface patterns, and develop a playbook. Otherwise every case depends on who handles the call.

(source: call-03-customer-infosec-halric.md)

## Gap 4: Solo-practitioner tier (structural)

**Surface**: Call 004 — [[iolanthe-marsk]] can afford ~20 gold/month; GSE's smallest retainer is 60 gold/month.

**Problem**: A "lighter tier for solo wizards" (remote monitoring, quarterly on-site, sending-stone access to on-call lead) has been discussed internally for six months with no product specs. Multiple intake reps describe it differently. Waitlist at ~14 people.

**Current state**: GSE can sell one-time assessments (100 gold) to this segment but cannot convert them to ongoing clients. The assessment-only path leaves prospects defended by their own implementation, with no ongoing relationship.

**Recommendation from rep notes**: Decide — build it (and tell prospects a credible timeline) or stop mentioning it. The current in-between is misleading to prospects and frustrating for the intake team.

(source: call-04-prospect-physical-iolanthe.md)

## Gap 5: Institutional clients (structural)

**Surface**: Call 005 — [[northwatch-academy]], the third institutional inquiry this year.

**Problem**: GSE's entire methodology — pricing, training, staffing, client communication — assumes an individual-wizard buyer who makes their own decisions. Institutional clients (academies, guilds, organizations) have:

- Committee-based decision-making
- External affiliations (e.g., Mage's Guild charter)
- Students/staff who are not direct clients
- Different failure modes (enrollment loss, political pressure, press adversaries)

Additionally, press/publication-focused reputation work (working against a specific newspaper) is outside GSE's current experience, which is oral-network / village-level.

**Recommendation from rep notes**: Strategic discussion needed — build an institutional practice or formally decline the market and refer out. Taking [[northwatch-academy]] on without deciding is "effectively saying yes to the first option by accident."

(source: call-05-prospect-pr-northwatch.md)

## Pattern analysis

Three of the five gaps are **structural** (policy, playbook, product) and two are **procedural** (reference docs, consistent messaging). The structural ones are more urgent because each successive call keeps hitting them. (source: gse-calls/README.md)

The gaps cluster by urgency:

- **Immediate friction**: Gaps 2 and 4 are causing real-time problems on calls (can't quote numbers, describing nonexistent products differently).
- **Strategic decisions needed**: Gaps 1, 3, and 5 require leadership direction before they can be resolved.
- **Compounding risk**: Gap 5 (institutional clients) is accumulating — three inquiries this year with no strategy. Each one taken on without a decision sets implicit precedent.
