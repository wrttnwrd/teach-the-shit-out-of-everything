# Northwatch Academy

**Summary**: Northwatch Academy of Evocation is a [[public-relations]] prospect of [[green-slaad-enterprises]]. An institutional client — the third institutional inquiry this year — facing a reputation crisis after a student tavern brawl escalated into press coverage and enrollment withdrawals.

**Sources**: call-05-prospect-pr-northwatch.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

## Profile

- **Type**: Academy of Evocation
- **Charter**: Chartered by the Mage's Guild, operates independently (financially, academically, disciplinarily). The Guild prefers not to be involved.
- **Leadership contact**: Master Tenric Vosh, Dean of Admissions
- **Enrollment**: 41 current students
- **Location**: Northwatch
- **Rep**: [[mirella-thane]]
- **Status**: Prospect — two-week assessment proposed, awaiting confirmation by Rainmoon 05

(source: call-05-prospect-pr-northwatch.md)

## The incident

Three third-year students were involved in a tavern brawl off-campus. They intervened (they say defensively). Result: significant property damage and one broken arm (not theirs). Town watch interviewed and released them. No charges. Tavern owner compensated. Officially, the matter is closed.

### Escalation

The Northwatch Courier ran an unsigned column: "When Is A School Not A Menace?" The piece cited the tavern incident plus three other minor events spanning two years. Consequences:

- A town-council petition against the academy
- Four withdrawal letters from parents (out of 41 students — significant)
- The academy is "not in a strong position to absorb it"

(source: call-05-prospect-pr-northwatch.md)

## What the academy wants

1. **Short-term**: The Courier stops running pieces. The petition goes away.
2. **Medium-term**: Get through next admissions cycle without further enrollment loss.
3. **Long-term**: A more constructive presence in the town.

Mirella assessed these as three different timelines and three different projects. (source: call-05-prospect-pr-northwatch.md)

## GSE's honest assessment

Mirella was direct about capability and limits:

**Within GSE's range:**

- Framing the academy's public narrative
- Training students and faculty on what to say in town
- Building a good-works program to change the base rate of tolerance

**Outside GSE's current experience:**

- Working against a specific publication (the Courier) — GSE's work is oral-network / village-level, not press-adversary work
- Managing institutional politics — the Guild's preferences, the board, faculty comfort with PR work. GSE's methodology assumes an individual-wizard buyer who makes their own decisions.

(source: call-05-prospect-pr-northwatch.md)

## Proposed engagement

A two-week paid assessment at 400 gold plus travel. Mirella would personally:

- Visit the dean's office
- Read the Courier's archives
- Walk the town
- Meet the tavern owner
- Listen

Deliverable: a written brief covering (1) what the actual problem is, (2) what GSE can do, and (3) whether GSE should be the firm doing it — with referral recommendations if not.

Mirella insisted on running the assessment personally ("too outside our normal pattern" for a junior). (source: call-05-prospect-pr-northwatch.md)

## Strategic significance

This is the third institutional inquiry GSE has received this year. The entire methodology, pricing, training, and staffing model assumes an individual wizard. Academies, guilds, and organizations have different politics, timelines, and failure modes. This engagement forces a strategic decision: build an institutional practice, or formally decline the market and refer out. See [[operational-gaps]].

Mirella's assessment: "If we take this engagement we will learn a great deal. That's an argument for taking it. It is also a reason to staff it carefully." Tenric Vosh is described as "sharp, forthright, and not trying to lowball us." (source: call-05-prospect-pr-northwatch.md)
