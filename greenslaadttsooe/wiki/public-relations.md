## Core principles

**The mob is the real threat.** 92% of tower losses over the past century came from commoner mobs, not magical attacks. PR is the primary defense against this threat. (source: gse-site/post-torches.md)

**The first story wins.** [[narrative-timing]] — Mirella's core methodology — holds that what people hear first becomes the frame, and everything heard afterward gets fitted to that frame. You cannot unmake a first impression; you can only race to establish it yourself. (source: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md)

**Acknowledge the fear, not the accusation.** Most wizards argue the accusation and leave the fear alone. GSE teaches the opposite: address why people are afraid, not the technical details of what did or didn't happen. (source: gse-site/post-cow.md)

**No lying.** GSE does not manufacture stories. They surface true stories that weren't being told. Spin based on falsehood always collapses. If the true thing is genuinely bad, they say so and advise accordingly. This is contractual, not aspirational. (source: gse-site/public-relations.md, gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md)

**No mind control.** In the contract in four places. (source: gse-site/public-relations.md)

## The cow incident

The defining case study for the PR practice. See [[the-cow-incident]] for the full analysis. In brief: a wizard was falsely accused of turning a child into a cow. His three instincts (refuse to engage, explain transmutation theory, cast a light spell) all made it worse. GSE's response — produce the child, acknowledge the fear, pay reparations, change the tavern song — saved the tower in hours. (source: gse-site/post-cow.md)

## Entry-level engagement

The **reputation audit** is positioned as the entry door to the PR practice. A one-week engagement, flat fee: bards visit every tavern within a two-day ride, listen, and report back unfiltered. No commitment to further work. "Then we'll decide together what to do about it." (source: gse-site/public-relations.md)

The underlying argument: "If you haven't heard what the village is saying about you, the village is saying something you wouldn't like." Surfacing that gap is almost always the prerequisite to any further PR work. (source: gse-site/public-relations.md)

## Education and content

GSE publishes a free [[crisis-pr-course]] — ten lessons taught by Mirella, adapted from internal staff training. No signup, no paywall. The firm's stated reasoning: educated clients are better clients. (source: gse-site/course-crisis-pr.md)

The firm also publishes blog posts on security and PR topics, available without subscription. (source: gse-site/blog.md)

## Known service gaps

- **No geographic coverage policy**: No written policy for engagements outside the Sword Coast. No tiered travel pricing. No vetted referral list. See [[operational-gaps]]. (source: call-01-customer-pr-archibald.md)
- **No institutional-client practice**: Methodology assumes an individual-wizard buyer. Academies, guilds, and organizations have different politics, staffing needs, and failure modes. Third institutional inquiry received this year. See [[operational-gaps]]. (source: call-05-prospect-pr-northwatch.md)
- **No press/publication-focused capability**: Most PR work is oral-network / village-level. Working against a specific publication (like the Northwatch Courier) is outside current experience. (source: call-05-prospect-pr-northwatch.md)
