## Incident — east wall intrusion attempt

Someone reached Delara's east wall during third watch. The ward triggered, the detail responded, but the intruder descended the wall and reached the treeline before visual contact. No physical contact occurred.

Assessment: ward and response systems worked as designed, but the intruder reached the wall unobserved, indicating an outer-perimeter coverage gap.

### Proposed upgrades

1. **Re-walk the east approach** — Full team to identify the perimeter gap. Scheduled immediately.
2. **Second ring of lower-threshold wards** — At thirty paces from the wall. Previously declined by Delara; now reconsidering. Cost proposal requested.
3. **Two-person standing watch** — Replace one-plus-on-call model. Response time was 4.5 minutes (within spec but slow end). Cost: ~70% increase on the guard line. Delara is considering.

(source: call-02-customer-physical-delara.md)

## Guard welfare inquiry

Delara asked directly about liability and benefits if a guard is seriously injured or killed on her site. [[corrin-vale]] could provide general answers (hazard acknowledgment at hire, indemnity fund, death benefit, two historical payouts) but could not cite specific figures — flagged as an [[operational-gaps|operational gap]].

Key points from what Corrin could share:

- Guards sign a hazard acknowledgment at hire.
- GSE carries an indemnity fund for serious injury and a death benefit, paid regardless of circumstances.
- Client has no liability if the guard was doing their job. Exposure exists only if the client failed to disclose relevant hazards (e.g., an undisclosed ward).
- Fund has paid out twice in GSE's history. GSE remains in contact with both families.
