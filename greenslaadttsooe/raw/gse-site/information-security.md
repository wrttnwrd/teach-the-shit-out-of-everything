## The threats, in plain terms

I'll skip the taxonomies and just tell you what we see most:

### Scrying

Someone, somewhere, is trying to watch you work. They want to learn your preparation rituals, your sleep schedule, and which component pouch you keep the rare stuff in. Our sweeps find about one active scrying effect per wizard per month. That number is not decreasing.[^2]

### Social engineering

A "messenger" arrives from a lord you've never met, with an urgent request you can't refuse, and all you have to do is send a quick reply with a very specific phrase in it. That phrase is a component. You just cast something on yourself for free. Don't.

### Supply-chain attacks

Your component supplier's apprentice is bribed. Now every batch of diamond dust comes with a sympathetic link back to a scrying pool. We inspect your supply chain. We replace your supply chain. We become your supply chain, if you want.

### Insider threats

This is the hard one. It's almost always the apprentice you trusted, and it's almost never malicious — they just told the wrong cousin at a wedding. We do background checks, ongoing trust audits, and what we politely call "lifestyle monitoring." Your staff will know we're doing it. That's the point.
