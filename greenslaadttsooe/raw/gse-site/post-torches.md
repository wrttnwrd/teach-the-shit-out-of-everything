# Your Wards Won't Save You From Torches

_**Physical Security** · Frostmoon 14_

> **TL;DR** Ninety-two percent of tower losses in the last hundred years came from human mobs, not magical attacks. Stop optimizing for the threat that sounds cool. Optimize for the threat that's actually happening.

I'm going to tell you something uncomfortable, and I'd like you to sit with it before you argue.

We went through a hundred years of incident reports. Every tower loss we could document. Every burned sanctum, every sacked library, every "the wizard just disappeared and we're not asking questions."[^1] We sorted them by cause of loss.

Here's what we found:

- **Mobs of commoners:** 92 incidents
- **Rival spellcasters:** 4 incidents
- **Extraplanar entities:** 2 incidents
- **Dragons:** 1 incident, and that dragon was hired by a mob of commoners
- **Unknown/other:** 1 incident

Ninety-two out of one hundred. Farmers. With torches.

## Where wizards spend their defense budget

Now here's the thing that really gets me. We also have records of what those wizards _spent their money on_ before they were killed. Approximately:

- **Anti-scrying defenses:** 40%
- **Magical wards against other casters:** 35%
- **Planar defenses:** 15%
- **Physical security and reputation work:** 10%

Ninety percent of the budget against ten percent of the actual threat. Ten percent of the budget against ninety percent of the actual threat.[^2]

If a merchant ran their stall this way, they'd be broke by Highharvest. But wizards do it routinely, and nobody in the industry talks about it, because the industry makes most of its money selling anti-scrying wards.

## Why it happens

I have theories. None of them flatter the profession.

**Theory one: the threat model is self-flattering.** If your enemy is a rival archmage, then you are, by implication, interesting enough to be a rival archmage's problem. If your enemy is the miller's wife who thinks you looked at her funny at market day, you are a _nuisance._ Wizards would rather be a target than a nuisance. I understand this. I think it's killing them.

**Theory two: the defenses against rival casters are fun to design.** Ward glyphs, counterspells, false sanctums — these are interesting problems. Hiring a bard to hang around the village tavern and listen for grumbling is not an interesting problem. It's just work. Wizards do not like work that is not interesting.

**Theory three: nobody wants to admit that peasants can kill them.** The whole identity of the archmage rests on being untouchable. To accept that a committee of angry farmers is a real threat is to accept that you are, fundamentally, touchable. Most wizards would rather die than accept that.[^3]

## What to do instead

I'm not going to turn this into a sales pitch. The pitch is on [our services page](physical-security.md), and the [PR practice](public-relations.md) page, and you can read it at your leisure. But here's the free advice:

1. **Rebalance your defense budget.** If you're not spending at least a third of it on physical security and reputation work, you are defending against the wrong threat.
2. **Know who lives within a day's walk of your tower.** By name. The innkeeper. The priest. The blacksmith. You don't have to like them. You have to know them.
3. **Fix something in the village, visibly, every season.** A well. A roof. A sick cow. Don't explain how you did it. Just do it.
4. **Stop dressing like a threat.** I cannot tell you how many wizards show up at market day in full ritual robes and then are surprised when the villagers get nervous. This is not a difficult problem. Wear a tunic.
