**SERA:** Halric, it's Sera. Calling about your quarterly sweep. Looking at Frostmoon 22 or 23 — can your household accommodate either?

**HALRIC:** The 23rd is better. We have a delivery on the 22nd and I'd rather not have two sets of strangers in the sanctum in the same day.

**SERA:** 23rd it is. I'll block the whole morning. Same team as last quarter?

**HALRIC:** Please. The new one — Veda? — was good. The junior one.

**SERA:** Veda. I'll tell her, she'll be pleased.

**HALRIC:** Before you go. Two things.

**SERA:** Go.

**HALRIC:** One. I had a sending last week that looked wrong. Said it was from my cousin. Had the right cadence, even had the private joke he uses when he wants me to know it's really him. Nearly fooled me. I didn't respond. But — how did they get the joke?

**SERA:** (pause) That's the kind of question I want you calling about. Good instinct. The honest answer is, probably from your cousin. Either directly — he told someone, or someone overheard — or indirectly, from someone who's seen your correspondence. How long has the joke been in use?

**HALRIC:** Years.

**SERA:** Then the leak could be old. I'd like to do a quick audit of his end. Does he know he has the leak?

**HALRIC:** He does not.

**SERA:** Awkward conversation, but worth having. We can do the audit on his side if he'll let us — it's a half-day engagement, we bill it to you or him, your call. In the meantime, pick a new signal with him. Something you decide together on a single trip, in person, nowhere near a sending stone. Use it going forward. If someone tries the old joke, you'll know.

**HALRIC:** Good. Do that.

**SERA:** Second thing?

**HALRIC:** _[⚑ Gap question]_ This is — this is more awkward. My apprentice. I've had her two years. She's good. But last month I noticed some of my preparation notes had been — read. Moved. Put back slightly wrong. Not stolen. Read.

**SERA:** Mm.

**HALRIC:** I don't want to accuse her. I don't want to fire her. But I can't have it continuing. What do you do in this situation?

**SERA:** This is an area where I want to be careful with my answer, because the honest truth is we don't have a clean playbook for it. The infosec work is easier — I can sweep for scrying, I can put a monitored decoy out, I can tell you within a few weeks whether she's the one reading. That part we can do.

**HALRIC:** And if she is?

**SERA:** That's where it gets harder. That's not really my field. It's closer to what Mirella's side does — relationship management, how to confront without escalating, how to either rebuild trust or part ways on terms that don't leak your information afterwards. If you want, I can loop her in.

**HALRIC:** I'd rather not loop more people in than I have to.

**SERA:** I understand. Let me say the honest thing. We can find out. I'm less confident in our advice on what to do next. We've handled maybe six of these. We've gotten good outcomes in some, not in others. I can tell you what we've learned but I don't have a playbook I can hand you.

**HALRIC:** That's fair.

**SERA:** Let's start with the diagnostic. Add a day to the sweep on the 23rd — we'll set the decoy, I'll brief you on what to watch for. Depending on what we find, we'll decide next steps together.

**HALRIC:** Alright.

**SERA:** Halric. You're doing the right thing by not jumping to conclusions. Most wizards in this situation either ignore it or fire someone on instinct. Both are worse than what you're doing.

**HALRIC:** Thank you, Sera.
