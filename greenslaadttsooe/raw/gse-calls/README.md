## Gap summary — what leadership should review

1. **Geographic coverage policy (Call 001).** We have no written policy on engagements outside the Sword Coast. No tiered travel pricing. No vetted referral list for out-of-region work. Mirella referred from memory.

2. **Guard indemnity and death-benefit specifics (Call 002).** Corrin could not quote specific numbers for the death benefit or indemnity fund when a client asked directly. This should be a reference every physical-security lead can cite from memory. Operations needs to produce a one-page.

3. **Insider-threat playbook (Call 003).** We have handled roughly six confirmed insider-leak cases with mixed outcomes and no written retrospective. Every engagement is currently first-principles work. Recommend an internal project to write up the pattern.

4. **Solo-practitioner tier (Call 004).** We have been mentioning a "lighter tier for solo wizards" to prospects for six months without product specs. Multiple intake reps describe it differently. Waitlist is at ~14 people. Decide: build it, or stop mentioning it.

5. **Institutional clients (Call 005).** Third institutional inquiry this year. Our entire methodology assumes an individual-wizard buyer. Academies, guilds, and other organizations have different politics, different staffing needs, and different failure modes. Strategic decision needed: build an institutional practice, or formally decline the market and refer out. Taking Northwatch on without deciding is an accidental yes to the first.

Three of the five gaps are structural (policy, playbook, product) and two are procedural (reference docs, consistent messaging). The structural ones are more urgent because each successive call keeps hitting them.
