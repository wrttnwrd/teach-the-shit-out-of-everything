# Brief: Minimum Viable Security For A Wizard On A Budget

**Answers questions from questions.md:** Q20 (assembled DIY checklist); touches Q3 (Iolanthe's "20g/month") and Q22 (do I need a professional).

**Size / type:** Teach-small · New stuff (GSE's advice is scattered across four posts; never collected).

## Reader

Solo practitioners below GSE's 60g/month retainer floor. [[iolanthe-marsk]] is the archetype: one tower, one housekeeper, no guards, likes the village, small budget.

## Concrete problem manifestations

- You've read `post-torches.md` and know you're defended against the wrong threat.
- You're a working diviner, not an archmage.
- You've taken the course and can't buy a retainer on top.

## Brand-agnostic teaching (biggest part)

Organize by cost tier.

**Free, starting today:**

- Know your neighbors by name — innkeeper, priest, blacksmith, miller. Recognition, not friendship.
- Don't dress like a threat at market day. Wear a tunic.
- Fix something visibly every season. No explanation required.
- Rotate sending-stone signals in person. (See the companion brief on sending-stone hygiene.)
- Apprentice training: the five tavern rules (location, research, schedule, leave, report).

**Low-cost, once a year:**

- One professional assessment (100g / two-day model). Most diviners in Iolanthe's position implement 90% of the brief themselves. That's fine.

**Low-cost, ongoing:**

- Rebalance defense budget toward physical security and PR — even 20g/month reallocated from anti-scrying.
- Formalize the good-works you already do. Iolanthe already delivers babies; the program isn't *doing* more, it's making sure the village knows.

**What to cut (the counter-intuitive part):**

- Anti-scrying is 40% of the average wizard's budget against ~4% of actual tower losses.
- Custom warding glyphs against rival casters — 4 losses out of 100 in a century.
