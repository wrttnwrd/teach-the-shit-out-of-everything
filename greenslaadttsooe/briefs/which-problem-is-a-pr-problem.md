# Brief: Which Of Your Problems Is A PR Problem?

**Answers questions from questions.md:** The cross-practice triage gap (implicit across all five call transcripts; behind several entries in questions.md but not framed there directly).

**Size / type:** Teach-small · New stuff. Unusual positioning — competitors sell single services and can't teach this without cannibalizing themselves. GSE can, and that is the point.

## Reader

Wizards confronting an unfamiliar situation who don't know whether they have an infosec problem, a physical security problem, or a PR problem. Often all three.

## Concrete problem manifestations

- Apprentice is acting strangely — reading your notes, knowing things they shouldn't. Infosec? PR? Physical?
- The village is quieter than usual when you visit — brewing PR problem, or is someone casing your tower and intimidating witnesses?
- You've been scammed — is it over, or does it become a reputation crisis when the story leaks?

## Brand-agnostic teaching (biggest part)

Three diagnostic questions, in order:

1. **Is anyone getting hurt, or about to?** → Physical first. Everything else waits.
2. **Is information moving in a direction you didn't choose?** → Infosec. Includes notes read, sendings spoofed, suppliers leaking, scryings active.
3. **Is a story about you moving through a human network?** → PR. Includes village gossip, anonymous columns, guild talk, peer-order rumors.

**How they overlap — the honest section, which is the teaching:**

- The cow incident was ~80% PR, but the gathering mob required PS posture, and the accusation's seed could easily have been an infosec leak.
- The `post-scam.md` case where a mis-routed sending completed scrying triangulation started as infosec, became physical.
- An apprentice reporting suspicious tavern questions is infosec upstream, PR downstream.

**The triage rule:** start with the practice that owns "people getting hurt," move to the practice that contains the next irreversible action, then to the practice that shapes the long-term story.

## Where GSE fits (light)

Closing — GSE runs all three practices precisely so the triage doesn't bounce between firms. One intake call. Framed as operational reality, not sales pitch.

## What to avoid

- A 15-node flowchart. The teaching is three questions in order.
- Implying clean separation. Real crises are mixed, and saying so is the whole point.
- Framing this as a way to pick the *cheapest* service. It's a way to pick the *right* first move.
