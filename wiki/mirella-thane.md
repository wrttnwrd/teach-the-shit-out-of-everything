# Mirella Thane

**Summary**: Head of Public Relations at [[green-slaad-enterprises]]. Thirty years in reputation management. Former court bard, royal herald, and independent consultant. Architect of the firm's PR methodology.

**Sources**: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md, gse-site/public-relations.md, gse-site/course-crisis-pr.md, gse-site/post-cow.md, call-01-customer-pr-archibald.md, call-05-prospect-pr-northwatch.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

## Current role

Mirella has led the GSE [[public-relations]] practice since its founding — fifteen years. Responsibilities include:

- **Crisis response**: She or one of her seconds is deployed within an hour of any active incident.
- **Reputation audits**: She designed the methodology and personally runs audits above a certain risk threshold.
- **Bard retainer program**: Recruits, trains, and manages the bard network that handles ongoing narrative work.
- **Training curriculum**: All internal PR training originated from her lecture notes.

She personally leads a small number of engagements per year. Her team handles the rest to her standards, with her review. (source: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md)

## Career history

### Court bard to House Vareth (8 years)

Took the post at sixteen — unusually young. Held it through three successions and two wars of contested inheritance. Actual role was chief communications officer: drafting announcements, briefing heralds, managing what the countryside was told and in what order. Left after declining to lie about a duke's death. The house survived the truth. She considers this the most important lesson of her early career. (source: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md)

### Royal herald, Kingdom of Aldoran (6 years)

Responsible for official proclamations across seventeen provinces. Built the messenger network. Negotiated cross-border announcement protocols with four neighboring kingdoms. Personally delivered the coronation proclamation of Queen Aethelind — announced simultaneously in three cities via three heralds reading identical scripts she wrote. Developed [[narrative-timing]] during this period. (source: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md)

### Independent consultant (11 years)

Private consultant to noble houses and arcane practitioners. Notable engagements:

- Three noble heirs cleared of poisoning accusations within a season (no trials required).
- An abjurer cleared of summoning a fiend — the fiend was an illusion cast by a rival, whom Mirella produced within two weeks.
- A Lady Castellan accused (correctly) of using arcane means to fix grain prices. Mirella reframed it as price stabilization during a famine. The Castellan kept her position.

Came to GSE fifteen years ago because "the wizards needed it more, and the noble houses had each other to lean on." (source: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md)

## Methodology and teaching

Key written works:

- **The Cow-Incident Playbook** — 17 pages, internal, updated annually.
- **The Three Frames** — Core teaching on how accusations get shaped in public memory. Required reading for all PR staff. Taught in Lesson 04 of the [[crisis-pr-course]].
- **On Not Lying** — Monograph arguing spin based on falsehood always collapses. Distributed free to clients.
- **Tavern Listening** — Curriculum for bards joining the retainer program. She still teaches the opening session personally.

She lectures twice yearly at the College of Arms and once yearly at the Guild of Heralds. (source: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md)

## Working style

- Will not lie for clients. Finds the true thing worth telling and ensures it's told well and first.
- Runs crises calmly — described as asking about the miller's gout while the mob is at the gate.
- Direct — will tell clients when their instincts are wrong.
- Reads everything before a meeting — published work, regional gossip, local proclamations, ballads.

Still performs music occasionally at a tavern in the Old Quarter under an unpublished name. Self-described "middling lute player" and "first-rate listener." (source: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md)
