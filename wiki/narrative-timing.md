# Narrative Timing

**Summary**: [[mirella-thane]]'s core methodology for reputation management. The principle that what people hear first becomes the frame, and everything heard afterward gets fitted to that frame. Developed during her tenure as Royal Herald of Aldoran.

**Sources**: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md, gse-site/post-cow.md, gse-site/course-crisis-pr.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

## The principle

> What people hear first becomes the frame, and everything they hear afterward gets fitted to that frame. You cannot unmake a first impression. You can only race to establish it yourself.

(source: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md)

This observation underlies the entire GSE [[public-relations]] practice. It explains both why crises escalate (the accusation sets the frame before the wizard responds) and how they're resolved (establishing a new frame before the old one fully sets).

## Origin

Mirella developed the narrative-timing concept during her six years as Royal Herald of the Kingdom of Aldoran. She was responsible for official proclamations across seventeen provinces and built the messenger network that carried them. The practical challenge — ensuring the crown's version of events reached the countryside before rumor did — became the theoretical foundation.

The defining test was the coronation proclamation of Queen Aethelind during a disputed succession. The proclamation had to be announced simultaneously in three cities by three different heralds reading identical scripts. Mirella wrote the scripts. The lesson: timing and consistency of the first message are more important than the message's content. (source: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md)

## Application in crisis response

In [[the-cow-incident]], narrative timing operated at two levels:

1. **The accusation set the frame first.** By the time Archibald responded (18 hours later), the "wizard turned a child into a cow" story was already the only story in the tavern. His attempts to explain transmutation theory didn't replace the frame — they were interpreted through it ("he has a cow-magic book").

2. **GSE raced to set a new frame.** A bard was in the tavern the next night with a song about Archibald healing a sick goat. The new story was in the air before the old one finished traveling. The goal was not to deny the old story but to displace it with a true one.

(source: gse-site/post-cow.md)

## The Three Frames

Mirella's related teaching, covered in Lesson 04 of the [[crisis-pr-course]]: every accusation gets shaped into one of three frames in public memory. The course teaches how to spot which frame is forming and reshape it before it sets. The specific three frames are documented in GSE's internal materials (not published in the sources reviewed). (source: gse-site/course-crisis-pr.md)

## Related concepts

Narrative timing intersects with several other GSE principles:

- **[[golden-hour]]** — The operational framing of narrative timing in the first minutes of a crisis. Narrative timing explains *why* the first frame wins; the Golden Hour names the specific window during which you can still be the one setting it. (source: raw/5 Steps for Navigating the First Hour of a Crisis.md)
- **"Acknowledge the fear, not the accusation"** — Both operate on the same insight: the emotional frame matters more than the factual content. (source: gse-site/post-cow.md)
- **"On Not Lying"** — Narrative timing works with true stories, not fabricated ones. The goal is to establish the right true story first, not to manufacture a false one. (source: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md)
- **Bard retainer program** — The operational mechanism for narrative displacement at regional scale. Bards adjust which verses get emphasized, not whether the song is true. (source: gse-site/public-relations.md)

## Connection to broader frameworks

The Reddit discussion on [[scrying]] references Alfred Hitchcock's theory of suspense vs. surprise, which a commenter connected to the question of whether to reveal scrying to players. The parallel to narrative timing: suspense (knowing something is happening) creates engagement; surprise (revealing it later) creates a flat reaction. Mirella's methodology favors controlling the first story over preserving secrets. (source: raw/Do The Players Know They Are The Target of Scry?.md)

The Forbes article on introverted entrepreneurs and PR contains a parallel insight from Alex Furman: what felt like "inauthentic communication" became "storytelling" once the frame shifted from self-promotion to sharing a story worth telling. This mirrors Mirella's approach — the PR work is surfacing true stories, not performing false ones. (source: Nine Tips To Help Introverted Entrepreneurs Tackle PR Confidently And Successfully.md)
