# Physical Security

**Summary**: GSE's physical security service provides bodyguards, ward calibration, sanctum hardening, travel escorts, and rapid response teams for arcane practitioners.

**Sources**: gse-site/physical-security.md, gse-site/post-torches.md, gse-site/post-divination.md, call-02-customer-physical-delara.md, call-04-prospect-physical-iolanthe.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

## Service offerings

| Service | Description |
|---|---|
| Personal bodyguard detail | One to four guards on twelve-hour shifts. All are retired adventurers. |
| Tower warding | Layered alarm, glyph of warding, and custom trigger-chains calibrated to the client's daily routine. |
| Sanctum hardening | Physical reinforcement of doors, windows, and secret passages. GSE claims to always find a secret passage. |
| Travel escort | Mounts, routes, decoy caravans. Teleportation available but discouraged due to teleport sickness. |
| Rapid response | Sending-stone link to on-call team. Average response time: eleven minutes. |

(source: gse-site/physical-security.md)

## Standard engagement model

1. **Threat assessment** — Two days on-site. Walk the tower, meet staff, read omens, talk to villagers.
2. **Recommendation brief** — Plain-language, no jargon, no upsells. Clear cost breakdown.
3. **Deployment** — Guards on rotation, wards laid, response line open.
4. **Quarterly review** — Ongoing reassessment as threats change.

(source: gse-site/physical-security.md)

## The case for physical security

GSE's data on tower losses is the core argument for this service line. Over 100 years of incident reports:

- Mobs of commoners: 92 incidents
- Rival spellcasters: 4
- Extraplanar entities: 2
- Dragons: 1 (hired by commoners)
- Unknown: 1

Yet wizards spend roughly 90% of their defense budget on anti-scrying and magical wards — threats that account for ~10% of actual losses. GSE argues the budget should be inverted. (source: gse-site/post-torches.md)

The firm also argues that [[scrying]] and divination cannot substitute for physical security. Divination answers questions the wizard thought to ask; it does not notice threats they didn't anticipate, does not run while they sleep, and operates on the wrong timescale to stop an in-progress attack. (source: gse-site/post-divination.md)

## Ethical boundaries

- No expendable guards — guards are expected to dodge arrows, not just absorb them.
- No necromancer hires — undead guards are cheap, unreliable, and a PR disaster.
- No dragon guarantees — the firm will not claim to defend against dragons.

(source: gse-site/physical-security.md)

## Guard welfare

GSE carries an indemnity fund for serious guard injuries and a death benefit for families, paid regardless of incident cause. The fund has paid out twice in the firm's history. Specific figures are not documented in a referenceable format — this is a known [[operational-gaps|operational gap]]. (source: call-02-customer-physical-delara.md)
