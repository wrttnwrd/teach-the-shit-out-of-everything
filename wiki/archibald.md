# Archibald

**Summary**: Transmuter and [[public-relations]] retainer client of [[green-slaad-enterprises]] since Frostmoon 02. Central figure in [[the-cow-incident]], the firm's defining case study.

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

## Profile

- **Discipline**: Transmutation
- **Client since**: Frostmoon 02
- **Service**: Public Relations retainer (quarterly check-ins)
- **Rep**: [[mirella-thane]]
- **Status**: Stable. Retainer extended through next quarter at current level.

"Archibald" is a pseudonym used in published materials — not his real name, and reportedly he hates it. He gave GSE permission to publish [[the-cow-incident]] case study under this name. (source: gse-site/post-cow.md)

## The cow incident

Archibald was accused by his village of turning a local child into a cow. He had not done so. (He was actually trying to turn himself into a cow, for research — an entirely different situation GSE did not discuss with the villagers.) His three instincts — refusing to engage, explaining transmutation theory, and casting a light spell — all escalated the crisis. GSE's response saved the tower. See [[the-cow-incident]] for the full timeline. (source: gse-site/post-cow.md, gse-site/public-relations.md)

In his own words on [[mirella-thane]]: "She is the rudest person who has ever saved my life, and I would hire her again tomorrow." (source: gse-site/bio-mirella-thane.md)

## Current situation (Call 001)

At the most recent quarterly check-in, Archibald reported that the goat-healing song GSE seeded (part of the cow-incident recovery) had been too successful — nine weeks later, villagers were still singing it and inviting him to inspect their goats. He had been invited to inspect three goats in one week; one wasn't even sick.

Mirella recommended seeding a second positive story (harvest-blessing angle) to displace the goat narrative over ~6 weeks, rather than extending the song's geographic range (which would just "move the goats around").

Archibald also asked about service for his sister, a practitioner in the Free Cities who had experienced an incident. This surfaced GSE's lack of a geographic coverage policy — no written policy, no tiered travel pricing, no vetted referral list for out-of-region work. Mirella offered to assess the sister's situation and refer out if GSE couldn't serve her well. (source: call-01-customer-pr-archibald.md)
