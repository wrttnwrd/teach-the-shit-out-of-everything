## Issue 1 — Spoofed sending

Halric received a sending that appeared to be from his cousin, including the correct private joke used as an authentication signal. He did not respond — good instinct.

Sera's analysis: the joke was likely leaked from the cousin's side, either directly (told someone, overheard) or indirectly (seen in correspondence). The leak could be old since the joke has been in use for years.

### Actions taken

- Cousin-side audit offered as a separate half-day engagement (billable to Halric or cousin).
- Advised: establish a new shared signal, created together in person, away from any sending stone. Use it going forward; if someone tries the old joke, it's a confirmed compromise.

(source: call-03-customer-infosec-halric.md)

## Issue 2 — Suspected insider threat (apprentice)

Halric's apprentice of two years appears to have been reading his preparation notes. Notes were moved and put back slightly wrong — not stolen, but read. Halric does not want to accuse or fire her, but cannot allow it to continue.

### GSE approach

- **Diagnostic (within capability)**: Sera will set a monitored decoy during the Frostmoon 23 sweep to determine whether the apprentice is the one reading the notes.
- **Resolution (acknowledged gap)**: If confirmed, advising on next steps is harder. This crosses into relationship management ([[mirella-thane]]'s domain). Sera was transparent that GSE has handled ~6 similar cases with mixed outcomes and no written playbook.
- Halric declined to loop in additional people. Sera respected this.

This case is one of the evidence points behind the insider-threat playbook gap. See [[operational-gaps]].

(source: call-03-customer-infosec-halric.md)
