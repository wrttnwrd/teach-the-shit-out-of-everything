# Crisis PR Course

**Summary**: "Crisis PR For Wizards: How To Keep Your Tower When The Village Shows Up" — a free ten-lesson course taught by [[mirella-thane]], adapted from GSE's internal staff training. No signup, no paywall.

**Sources**: gse-site/course-crisis-pr.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

## Overview

A ten-lesson course on reputation management during crisis situations, available at no cost. Each lesson includes a video (10-25 minutes) and a written companion with examples, templates, and excerpts from Mirella's cow-incident playbook. Approximately four hours total.

No login, no email, no account required. Lessons are at stable URLs. The content is the same internal training Mirella gives to new GSE staff, adapted for a wizard audience. (source: gse-site/course-crisis-pr.md)

## Target audience

Practicing wizards with a tower, sanctum, or aggressively warded room above an apothecary. The concern is not a break-in (they can handle that magically) but the other threat: villagers gathering on the green, accusations set to music before breakfast, the bailiff at the door with questions. (source: gse-site/course-crisis-pr.md)

## The ten lessons

| # | Title | Length | Core teaching |
|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | The First Hour Decides The Rest | 18 min | The opening sixty minutes matter more than the next six weeks. Three things to do; three not to. See [[golden-hour]] for the underlying concept and the four-step framework adapted from PRSA. |
| 02 | Stop Explaining. Produce Evidence. | 14 min | Explanation extends a panic; evidence ends one. See [[the-cow-incident]]. |
| 03 | Acknowledge The Fear, Not The Accusation | 22 min | Two different things. Most wizards argue the accusation and leave the fear alone. Do the opposite. |
| 04 | The Three Frames | 25 min | Mirella's core teaching. Every accusation gets shaped into one of three frames. Learn to spot which is forming and reshape it. See [[narrative-timing]]. |
| 05 | On Not Lying | 19 min | Spin based on falsehood always collapses. What honest reframing looks like. Where the line is. |
| 06 | The Ritual Of Reparation | 16 min | Symbolic gestures land harder than expected. How to choose them without looking like an admission of fault. |
| 07 | Changing The Song In The Tavern | 21 min | Narrative displacement through bards, barn-raisings, and the right verses. Not spin — surfacing true stories. |
| 08 | When The Accusation Is True | 24 min | The hardest chapter. Managing a crisis of your own making without losing practice, freedom, or self-respect. |
| 09 | Building Reputation Before You Need It | 17 min | The healed gout, the fixed well. The cheapest PR investment is the one made in a crisis-free year. |
| 10 | A Post-Mortem, Done Right | 15 min | How to extract real lessons, update the playbook, and avoid the version where you congratulate yourself and learn nothing. |

(source: gse-site/course-crisis-pr.md)

## Lessons recommended for apprentices

Lessons 02, 03, and 09 are specifically recommended for apprentice viewing. (source: gse-site/course-crisis-pr.md)

## Business rationale

GSE's stated reasoning: educated clients are better clients. If someone takes the course and never hires GSE, the firm considers it a good trade — fewer of the mistakes their peers charge to clean up. The honest catch: this is a reputation-management practice, and the course demonstrates competence. (source: gse-site/course-crisis-pr.md)

The course material is the same content GSE charges clients four figures to receive in person. (source: gse-site/course-crisis-pr.md)
