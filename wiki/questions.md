# Questions

**Summary**: Audience questions surfaced from source materials — questions our audience is asking, or would ask, that we can potentially answer.

**Last updated**: 2026-04-20

## Questions from call transcripts

These are real questions asked by clients and prospects during documented calls.

### Physical security

1. **What do I do if someone reaches my perimeter but escapes before my detail can respond?** (Delara Venn, Call 002) — Partially answered: re-walk approach, add outer ward ring, consider standing watch upgrade. No generalized guidance doc exists.

2. **If a guard is injured or killed on my site, who is liable? What happens to their family?** (Delara Venn, Call 002) — Answer exists but is not documented in a citable format. Gap flagged. See [[operational-gaps]].

3. **What can I get for 20 gold a month?** / **Is there a lighter tier for solo practitioners?** (Iolanthe Marsk, Call 004) — No current product. Assessment-only option at 100 gold exists. Lighter tier discussed internally for 6 months, no specs. See [[operational-gaps]].

### Information security

4. **Someone sent a message that perfectly mimicked my cousin's private authentication signal. How did they get it?** (Halric Bren, Call 003) — Answered: likely leaked from cousin's side (direct or indirect). Recommended cousin-side audit and new in-person signal.

5. **My apprentice is reading my notes. How do I confirm it without accusing them, and what do I do if confirmed?** (Halric Bren, Call 003) — Diagnosis answered (decoy monitoring). Resolution not answered — no playbook exists. See [[operational-gaps]].

### Public relations

6. **Do you work outside the Sword Coast?** (Archibald, Call 001) — No formal answer. Case-by-case basis. No written policy, no travel pricing, no referral list. See [[operational-gaps]].

7. **The positive story you seeded worked too well — now I'm the goat guy. How do we fix that?** (Archibald, Call 001) — Answered: seed a second positive narrative to displace the first over ~6 weeks. Don't extend geographic range (just moves the problem).

8. **Can you handle PR for an institution (academy, guild) rather than an individual wizard?** (Northwatch Academy, Call 005) — Partially answered. Some capabilities transfer (narrative framing, training, good-works programs). Institutional politics and press-adversary work are outside current experience. No institutional practice exists. See [[operational-gaps]].

## Questions implied by content

These questions are not asked directly in the sources but are clearly what the content is designed to answer.

### From blog posts

9. **What's the real threat to my tower?** — Answered thoroughly in "Your Wards Won't Save You From Torches." Answer: mobs (92% of tower losses), not rival casters.

10. **Can my divination practice keep me safe?** — Answered in "Divination Is Not A Security Strategy." Answer: no — divination has question, attention, and timing problems that physical security does not.

11. **How do I spot scams?** — Answered in "If A Duke You've Never Met Needs Mithril, It's A Scam." Answer: three-con taxonomy (fake nobility, fake emergency, fake admirer) + meta-rule on irreversible decisions under time pressure.

12. **Is my apprentice a security risk?** — Answered in "Your Apprentice Is Your Biggest Security Hole." Answer: yes, but through normal social behavior, not malice. Train them with the five tavern rules.

13. **What should I do in the first hour of a reputation crisis?** — Answered in "The Cow Incident" and the [[crisis-pr-course]]. Answer: stop explaining, produce evidence, acknowledge the fear.

### From third-party sources

14. **Can a scrying target detect that they're being watched?** — Answered via spell reference and Reddit thread. Answer: not without See Invisibility, True Sight, or Detect Magic. The spell is designed for covert observation. See [[scrying]].

15. **How do introverted business owners handle PR?** — Addressed in Forbes article. Strategies: reframe PR as storytelling, focus on quality contacts over quantity, practice in advance, start small, delegate if needed. Parallels GSE's own approach to wizard clients who are reclusive by nature.

### Implied but not directly addressed in sources

16. **The accusation against me is actually true. What do I do?** — Lesson 08 of the [[crisis-pr-course]] is entirely about this ("When The Accusation Is True"). [[mirella-thane]]'s case work includes one actual ethical lapse where she counseled voluntary retirement and public acknowledgment. Answered in course material, but not surfaced as a standalone question anywhere on the site.

17. **What should my staff or apprentice do if I'm in a crisis and making bad decisions?** — [[the-cow-incident]] was saved because Archibald's apprentice called GSE when Archibald wouldn't. No guidance exists for the apprentice/staff side of a crisis — when to override the master, who to contact, what authority they have. The apprentice training ("Your Apprentice Is Your Biggest Security Hole") covers infosec rules but not crisis-escalation protocol.

18. **How do I protect my sending-stone communications?** — [[halric-bren]]'s spoofed sending raises this. [[sera-oak]]'s advice ("pick a new signal in person") is case-specific. Her own rep notes call for a "client-education piece on signal hygiene." No general guidance on communication security exists as a standalone resource. GSE's [[information-security]] page lists "warded communications" as a service but doesn't teach the basics.

19. **How do I vet my component suppliers for tampering?** — Supply-chain attacks are described as a threat category on the [[information-security]] page (bribing a supplier's apprentice to introduce sympathetic links). GSE offers to "inspect, replace, or become your supply chain." No self-service guidance on what to look for or how to audit a supplier independently.

20. **What's the minimum viable security I can do myself on a limited budget?** — Distinct from "what does GSE charge." The torches post gives four free recommendations: rebalance your defense budget, know who lives within a day's walk by name, fix something in the village every season, stop dressing like a threat. The divination post adds that divination is useful for travel planning and long-range assessment. These are scattered across posts but never assembled as a "do this yourself" checklist.

21. **How do I build a good relationship with my village before I need one?** — Lesson 09 of the [[crisis-pr-course]] ("Building Reputation Before You Need It") and the good-works program both address this. But most existing questions are reactive (crisis already happening). The proactive version — what to do when nothing is wrong yet — is the higher-leverage question and the one GSE's own content argues matters most.

22. **How do I know when I need professional security vs. handling it myself?** — GSE's one-time assessment (100 gold, two days) exists precisely because this question is common. [[iolanthe-marsk]] was told many diviners implement 90% of recommendations themselves. But the triage question — "do I need help?" — has no published answer. The assessment is the answer, but the question that leads someone to book one isn't addressed.

23. **I'm a recluse. I don't want to talk to villagers. Can PR work without me being involved?** — GSE's entire client base is introverted wizards. The bard-retainer model does the public-facing work so the wizard doesn't have to. The Forbes article's strategies (delegate, reframe as storytelling, start small) parallel this. But the question is never explicitly framed — it's assumed. In [[the-cow-incident]], Mirella's first instruction was "go inside" — the wizard's absence was part of the solution.

## Questions added 2026-04-19

### From the Golden Hour source (PRSA, Monique Farmer)

29. **Who is my designated crisis spokesperson, and have they been named *before* the crisis?** — The [[golden-hour]] framework treats this as the single most important pre-crisis decision. GSE source material assumes the wizard is the default; [[the-cow-incident]] is the argument against that default. No published guidance exists on how a solo wizard picks one.

30. **Do I have a holding statement drafted for the three most likely crises I'd face?** — A holding statement is a short, pre-cleared response that buys time without committing to details. The concept is introduced in [[golden-hour]] but GSE's own material doesn't mention it. Likely candidate for an upgrade to the [[crisis-pr-course]] or a standalone teach-small post.

31. **Before I go public, does my household know what's happening?** — The "communicate internally first" step in [[golden-hour]]. [[the-cow-incident]] demonstrates the failure mode by omission: the apprentice improvised the call to GSE because no protocol existed. Related to implied question 17.

### From the wizard regulation source (Reddit /r/dndnext)

32. **Am I registered where I need to be — and *not* registered where I shouldn't be?** — The registration-and-licensing model from [[wizard-regulation]]. GSE sources do not specify whether their clients are in a registration regime. Both over- and under-registration are risks.

33. **Does my local jurisdiction treat me as a level-5+ "watched" caster, or as a low-level "ignored" one? How can I tell?** — The threat-tier model from [[wizard-regulation]]. Self-assessment without tripping surveillance is a distinct skill. No GSE guidance exists.

34. **If my apprentice is offered a court position, should they take it?** — The "court wizardry and gilded cages" model from [[wizard-regulation]]. Intersects with [[sera-oak]]'s apprentice-training pipeline and the insider-threat question (Halric's notes). Structurally a farewell, but potentially a security gain — no published GSE view.

35. **Are GSE's services legal in every jurisdiction I travel through?** — Counter-scrying, warding against inspection, and narrative displacement all potentially collide with state regulation. Related to unanswered question 25 (regions GSE doesn't serve): the reverse case — services GSE sells that aren't legal where the client is.

36. **PR for a wizard sanctioned by their own peer order — is that in the [[crisis-pr-course]]?** — The Dragonlance/peer-regulation model from [[wizard-regulation]]. A censure by your own order is a distinct crisis shape from a villager accusation. Not covered in the course, not covered in any source. Would require [[mirella-thane]]'s case work to validate.

## Questions added 2026-04-20

37. **How can I detect a scrying sensor in my own sanctum?** — Related to [[scrying]] and question 14 (whether a target can detect being scried). The wiki covers the general answer (See Invisibility, True Sight, Detect Magic) but not the sanctum-specific variant: what a wizard can build into their own warded space — persistent detection wards, scheduled sweeps, alarm triggers — so they do not have to cast a detection spell manually each time. No standalone GSE guidance exists.
